About Canggu

A Beachside Paradise

Located on Bali's southwestern coast, Canggu is a dynamic coastal village that seamlessly blends the charm of rural Bali with a lively, modern spirit. This vibrant destination is celebrated for its renowned surf breaks, scenic rice paddies, thriving art scene, and an eclectic culinary landscape. With an inviting community and a laid-back atmosphere, Canggu is the perfect place to experience the essence of Bali's cultural diversity and natural beauty.

Things to Do in Canggu

Discover Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot, a jewel among Bali's celebrated attractions, is a mesmerizing sight, perched on a small rock and revealing its mystical charm as the tide recedes. It's during the enchanting sunsets that this temple truly comes to life, bathed in the warm, golden hues of the twilight sky, creating a breathtaking vista. Canggu's proximity, conveniently under 30 minutes away, provides a stress-free gateway to this iconic temple, saving you from the hustle of Bali's often congested roads and ensuring an unforgettable visit to this coastal wonder.

Explore Canggu's Vibrant Markets

Canggu offers a vibrant market scene, each with its unique charm. Sundays come alive with the Samadi Market, a small yet diverse hub for locally-made goods and delicious produce. On Saturdays and Sundays, the Love Anchor Market unfolds its covered expanse, featuring souvenirs and occasional hidden gems. For the grand affair, don't miss Old Man's Market on the last Saturday of the month, boasting an array of food stalls, second-hand treasures, and local start-ups. Whether you seek unique souvenirs or delicious treats, these markets are a shopper's delight, and don't forget to indulge in Samadi Market's delectable muffins and cookies! Remember to carry cash, just in case.

Surfing Adventures in Canggu

Bali's legendary surf allure draws enthusiasts from around the globe, and Canggu offers a fantastic launchpad for beginners. Batu Bolong Beach is a hub for surf instructors, where you can dive into the world of surfing for around $15 per hour. However, be mindful of the ocean's temperament; Canggu's waves can be quite energetic and unpredictable, especially on windy days. If you're an experienced surfer, the challenge might be just what you seek. Otherwise, consider exploring Uluwatu for arguably Bali's finest surfing conditions. And remember, always respect the ocean's mood it's best to stay ashore if it's upset.
