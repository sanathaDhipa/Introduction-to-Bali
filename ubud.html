About Ubud

A Cultural Oasis

Nestled in lush forests, Ubud is Bali's artistic and cultural epicenter. Its vibrant streets are alive with colorful Balinese ceremonies, while exquisite temples grace every corner. The town is a thriving hub for art, offering high-quality souvenirs and handcrafted treasures. As Bali's premier yoga and wellness destination, Ubud invites you to explore yoga flows, meditation, and diverse dining experiences, from plant-based eateries to world-class restaurants. Discover the heart of Bali's cultural richness in Ubud.

Things to Do in Ubud

Explore Tirta Empul Temple

Tirta Empul, also known as the Holy Spring Water Temple, is a must-visit cultural gem in Ubud. This temple is steeped in tradition and is a central site for Hindu religious ceremonies. Visitors have the unique opportunity to participate in a self-purification ritual using the spring water, following specific guidelines posted at the entrance, such as securing long hair and respecting menstrual restrictions. The entrance fee is 30,000Rp per person (approximately $2 US), and the temple is open daily from 7:00 am to 5:00 pm. Wander through the temple, immerse yourself in the rich culture, and admire the magnificent architecture that surrounds this spiritual haven in Ubud.

Monkey Forest

The Monkey Forest in Ubud is a top attraction, housing hundreds of playful monkeys and over 180 tree species. This beautiful and educational spot offers a spiritual connection with nature that delights tourists. Open daily from 8:30 am to 6:00 pm, the entrance fee is 50,000Rp per person (approximately $4 US). While interacting with the monkeys can be a memorable experience, exercise caution, as they are wild animals, and some visitors have experienced bites or confrontations.

Tegalalang Rice Fields

Tegalalang stands as the most popular among the rice fields in Ubud. While we made a quick stop en route to the coffee plantation for some scenic snapshots, we didn't explore the site. The entrance fee is just 10,000Rp (less than $1 US), with additional small donations expected as you traverse the bridges and pathways (typically totaling around $3-4 US). To relish the tranquil beauty of the Tegalalang rice fields without the crowds, plan an early visit. The site is open 24 hours, offering a serene escape into Bali's natural splendor.
